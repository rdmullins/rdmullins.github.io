What is the Number One Thing That Held You Back This Week? Why?

On a macro level, my single biggest challenge for the week was undoubtedly time management and struggling to find a rhythm that would work both with the coursework and my family life. In terms of the concepts we covered, however, my biggest stumbling block was wrapping my brain around state. It represents a huge paradigm shift from the step-by-step linear programming model I learned the last time I took a high-level CS course.

What Is One Thing You Would Like to Learn More About Regarding JavaScript (JSON, Data Structures), Bootstrap, or Atomic Design?

Expanding on my previous post, I would like to spend more time with state. I found myself during the Mind Reader development process thinking back to my final project in the Intro to Web Developing course, Pursuit of Trivia. While I remain proud of what I put together using what we'd covered in class, I definitely see potential to use state to cut down on the amount of traffic and reloads required by the page.

How Does In-Person Bootcamp Compare to Remote Onboarding?

Remote on-boarding served its purpose by 'easing' us in to the material and giving room for occasional collaboration during standups or outside stand-up time in Slack. There were definite inherent challenges; the biggest for me was trying to keep myself on task while reviewing the material. In-person, by contrast, has helped immensely with the focus factor. In fact I feel almost as though I'm having to triage to determine what is going to get my focus at a certain time. The biggest difference, however, has been the level of collaboration that comes with being in person. The model lends itself much better than remote to our being able to help each other and learn from one anothers' approaches.

What is your 'Why'?

I have practical and more abstract 'why's. On the practical level, I made the decision to apply for Bootcamp because my office of almost sixteen years was looking likely to close by year-end. After they cut my pay (but not my hours) it made the decision that much easier from a financial standpoint. Along those same lines, our daughters are fast approaching college age - I'm not exactly at a point in life where I can afford to go backwards salary-wise. However there was definitely a deeper reason at play. After the Intro to Web Design class ended back in the spring, I realized about a week later that I really missed it. There was something about the web programming experience - making something from nothing - that touched a creative side of myself that I had forgotten was there. I'm looking forward to getting even deeper into the concepts we were able to cover in the Intro class and I hope to continue to reestablish contact with the part of me that enjoys learning and building new things.
